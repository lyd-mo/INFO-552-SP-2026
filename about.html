About Me.

Hi, I'm Lydia.

I'm a UX designer and Master's student in Human-Computer Interaction and User Experience at Drexel University, currently based in Philadelphia. I am set to graduate this summer, 2026.

I became a UX designer because it drives me nuts when websites or products just don't work. What is that even about? Life is hard, your websites should not be.

Anyways, I love the full arc of UX. I enjoy talking to users to uncover what they actually need, then diving into Figma where I can dig in and work through the details until everything is right.

My path to UX has been a bit unconventional. I started with a degree in Public Health and spent several years working in product, but I kept gravitating toward the parts of my work that involved creativity and designing better experiences for people. Eventually, I realized that's what I wanted to be doing full-time.

I thrive in collaborative environments and I'm especially interested in opportunities where good design can improve people's daily lives, including spaces within retail, healthcare, or public service.
